Rome - The eternal city

Top Attractions

  • The Colosseum

    • The Colosseum is an ancient Roman amphitheater located in Rome, Italy. Built between 70-80 AD, it could hold up to 50,000 spectators and was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and dramas. It remains a symbol of Roman engineering and is one of the most visited landmarks in the world. Today, it stands partially ruined due to earthquakes and stone theft but is a significant historical site, offering tours that delve into its rich past.

  • The Vatican Museums

    • The Vatican Museums, located within Vatican City, contain some of the most important art and cultural treasures of the Catholic Church. Established in the early 16th century, the museums house famous masterpieces like Michelangelo's ceiling in the Sistine Chapel and Raphael's Rooms. Visitors can explore a vast collection that includes ancient Roman and Egyptian art, Renaissance paintings, and more. The museums attract millions of visitors annually, offering a journey through centuries of art and history.

  • Travel Tips

    • Best Time to Visit:
    Spring (April-June) and Fall (September-October) for pleasant weather and fewer tourists.
    Local Customs:
    Greet with "Buongiorno" (good morning) and "Grazie" (thank you). Dress modestly when visiting churches, including the Vatican.
    Transportation:
    Metro and buses are the best options for public transport. Walking is ideal for the city center. Taxis and ridesharing services are also available. Be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas.
