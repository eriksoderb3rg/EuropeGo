Paris - The City of Lights

Top Attractions

  • The Eiffel Tower

    The Eiffel Tower is an iconic iron structure located in Paris, France. Built by engineer Gustave Eiffel for the 1889 World's Fair, it stands 330 meters tall and was initially criticized by some for its design. However, it quickly became a global symbol of French engineering and architectural innovation. The tower has three observation levels, offering panoramic views of Paris, and is one of the most visited monuments in the world. Originally meant to be dismantled after 20 years, it was preserved due to its value for communications technology and has since become a beloved landmark.

  • The Louvre Museum

    The Louvre Museum, located in Paris, France, is the world's largest art museum and a historic monument. Originally a royal palace, it became a public museum in 1793 after the French Revolution. The Louvre houses over 35,000 works of art, including famous pieces like Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa and the ancient Greek statue Venus de Milo. Its collections span from antiquity to the 19th century, showcasing art, sculptures, and artifacts from various cultures. The museum is renowned for its vast and diverse collection, attracting millions of visitors each year.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit: Spring (April-June) and Fall (September-October) for mild weather and fewer crowds.

Local Customs: Greet with "Bonjour" (hello) and "Merci" (thank you). Dress smartly, especially in restaurants and churches. Tipping is included, but rounding up is appreciated.

Transportation: Metro: Fast and affordable; multi-day passes available. Walking: Ideal for exploring central areas. Bikes: Use the “Vélib” bike-share for short trips. Uber and taxis are available, but taxis cost more at night. Paris is best enjoyed at a relaxed pace, embracing its cultural charm.
