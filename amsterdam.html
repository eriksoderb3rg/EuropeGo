Amsterdam - The Venice of the North

Top Attractions

  • The Anne Frank House

    The Anne Frank House is a poignant museum dedicated to the Jewish diarist Anne Frank, who hid in the house during World War II. Visitors can explore the Secret Annex where Anne and her family lived in hiding, as well as learn about her life and legacy through exhibits that recount the horrors of the Holocaust.

  • The Rijksmuseum

    The Rijksmuseum is the largest and most prestigious museum in the Netherlands, housing a vast collection of Dutch art and history. It showcases masterpieces from the Golden Age, including works by Rembrandt, Vermeer, and Van Gogh. The museum's beautifully restored building and gardens are attractions in themselves.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit:

    Spring (April-May) to enjoy the famous tulip season, or Fall (September-October) for fewer tourists and pleasant weather.

  • Local Customs:

    Greet with "Hallo" (hello) and "Dank je" (thank you). Cycling is a major part of the culture, so be mindful of bike lanes. Tipping is appreciated but not mandatory, usually rounding up the bill.

  • Transportation:

    Trams and Buses: The public transport system is extensive and efficient. Cycling: Rent a bike to experience the city like a local. Walking: Ideal for exploring the central canal areas. Ferries: Free ferries across the IJ river to the north of the city.
